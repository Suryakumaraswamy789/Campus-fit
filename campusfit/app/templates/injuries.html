Common Gym Injuries & Prevention

Disclaimer: This information is for educational purposes only. Always consult with a qualified medical professional or physical therapist for diagnosis and treatment of any injury.

Lower Back Strain

Cause: Often caused by improper form during deadlifts or squats, lifting too heavy, or rounding the back.
Prevention: Always maintain a neutral spine. Engage your core muscles. Warm up properly and never ego-lift. Ask a mentor to check your form.

Shoulder Impingement

Cause: Occurs when the tendons of the rotator cuff become pinched. This is often due to poor form on overhead presses or lateral raises, or muscle imbalances around the shoulder.
Prevention: Strengthen your rotator cuff muscles with external rotation exercises. Avoid flaring your elbows on pressing movements. Ensure you have proper shoulder mobility before lifting overhead.

Runner's Knee (Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome)

Cause: A dull ache around the front of the kneecap, often caused by overuse from running or jumping. Weak quadriceps and hip muscles can also contribute.
Prevention: Strengthen the quadriceps and glutes. Always wear appropriate footwear. Avoid sudden, drastic increases in your training volume. Stretch your hamstrings and hip flexors regularly.

Wrist Strain

Cause: Common in pressing exercises like the bench press or overhead press when the wrist is bent back too far. Can also happen during curls with improper form.
Prevention: Keep your wrists straight and strong during lifts (your knuckles should point towards the ceiling). Use wrist wraps for very heavy sets if needed. Perform wrist flexibility and strengthening exercises.
